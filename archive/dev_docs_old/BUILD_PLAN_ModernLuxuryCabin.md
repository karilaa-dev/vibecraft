## Key Design Features

### Modern Luxury Elements
1. **Floor-to-Ceiling Windows**: 3-block tall glass on main facades
2. **Double-Height Entry**: Void over foyer creates drama
3. **Clean Geometry**: Rectangular forms, flat roof, minimal ornamentation
4. **Material Contrast**: White concrete + dark oak + glass
5. **Indoor-Outdoor Connection**: Deck, balcony, large windows
6. **Luxury Finishes**: Polished stone floors, quartz ceilings
7. **Strategic Skylights**: Natural light in bathrooms

### Window Strategy
- **Ground Floor SE Wall**: 6 blocks of floor-to-ceiling glass (great room)
- **Ground Floor SW Wall**: 4 blocks of floor-to-ceiling glass (great room)
- **Second Floor SE Wall**: 4 blocks of floor-to-ceiling glass (master bedroom)
- **Ground Floor NW Wall**: 2×2 kitchen window
- **Window-to-Wall Ratio**: ~30% (modern standard)
- **Frame Style**: Minimalist glass_pane (no visible frames)

### Lighting Plan
- **Interior Spacing**: Sea lanterns or glowstone every 8 blocks (hidden in ceiling)
- **Exterior Accent**: Lanterns on deck posts
- **Skylight Lighting**: Natural light supplement
- **Entry Feature**: Glowstone panel behind glass (modern sconce effect)
